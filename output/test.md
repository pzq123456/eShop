## 1 INTRODUCTION

## 1.1 Background

Smart  Site  Safety  System  (SSSS)  is  integrated  with  smart  and  innovative  technologies  for enhancement  of  on-site  safety  management.  According  to  the  Technical  Circular  (Works) No.3/2023  from  Development  Bureau,  the  components  that  make  up  the  system  include "Centralized Management Platform", "Digitized Tracking System for Site Plants, Powered Tools and Ladders", "Digitalized Permit-to-work System for High Risk Activities", "Hazardous Areas Access Control by Electronic Lock and Key System", "Unsafe Acts / Dangerous Situation Alert for Mobile Plant Operation Danger Zone", "Unsafe Acts / Dangerous Situation Alert for Tower Crane Lifting Zone", "Smart Monitoring Devices for Workers and Frontline Site Personnel", "Safety Monitoring System using Artificial Intelligence" , "Confined Spaces Monitoring System" and  "Automated  Monitoring".  Also,  safety  training  with  virtual  reality  (VR)  Technology  is required to be provided for construction workers as per this technical circular.

The Smart Site Management Platform is dedicated to the digital and intelligent management of the entire construction process. In accordance with Technical Circular (Works) No. 3/2023, this platform integrates various cutting-edge technologies, including 3D GIS, BIM, the Internet of Things (IoT), Artificial Intelligence (AI), and Virtual Reality (VR), forming a comprehensive regulatory and early warning management system. It encompasses multiple stages of engineering data collection, transmission, storage, monitoring, early warning, and analysis, aiming to achieve a thorough understanding of construction site information.

By  deeply  integrating  technologies  and  enabling  interconnectivity  among  terminal  data,  the platform can monitor vast amounts of information on the construction site in real time. Utilizing intelligent  analysis  and  visualization  tools,  it  provides  site  managers  with  an  integrated monitoring and early warning management platform. Its primary objective is to minimize safety risks,  assist  in  formulating  response  measures,  promptly  identify  safety  hazards  and  unsafe behaviours, and issue early warnings.

The platform offers comprehensive technical support for the construction site in aspects such as supervision, early warning, inspection, visualization, and digital management, thereby achieving a comprehensive management of elements like the construction environment, personnel, assets, safety, monitoring, alarms, and workflows.

## 1.5 Objectives

- a) Digitalized  Permit-to-Work System:  Implement a  digitized  permit-to-work  system for high-risk activities that facilitates online, real-time applications, issuance, and tracking of permits for activities such as work in confined spaces, electrical hazards, and hot work.
- b) Hazardous Areas Access Control: Establish an automated access control and warning system with electronic locks to prevent unauthorized access to hazardous areas, ensuring only authorized personnel can open secured entrances.
- c) Unsafe acts / dangerous situation alert for mobile plant operation danger zone: Install automated warning systems on mobile plants to alert operators and nearby personnel of encroachments into danger zones, enhancing safety during operations involving heavy machinery.
- d) Safety Training with Virtual Reality Technology: The program supports 1:1 restoration of  the  real  situation  on  site,  uses  accurate  and  efficient  physical  engine  parameter calculation,  and  provides  correct  and  complete  construction  operation  knowledge, processes, information and other data. The structure of the equipment can be queried and displayed in a variety of ways. It is convenient for training personnel to quickly master

- e) Smart  Monitoring  Devices:  Equip  all  workers  and  frontline  personnel  with  smart monitoring devices to enable location tracking, detect standstill periods, and provide alerts for unauthorized area access.
- f) AI Safety Monitoring System: Utilize AI detection and video analytics to monitor unsafe scenarios on-site, improving detection accuracy to at least 90% and providing regular training for personnel on system usage. It also provides downloadable water level statistics and triggers alerts in the system when levels exceed predefined thresholds, thereby enhancing the safety of the construction environment.

Using GIS technology, it accurately visualizes  this  location  within  a  3D  model  of  the  area,  providing  critical  support  to management for rapid site identification and timely follow-up actions.
- j) Digitized    Tracking System: This system integrates spatial data and links information across  the  entire  asset  lifecycle  to  build  a  unified  "location-status-risk"  management platform. By solving the problems of information fragmentation and unpredictable risks inherent  in  traditional  methods,  it  provides  data-driven  support  for  on-site  safety  and resource  optimization. By implementing these strategies, the Smart Site Safety System will significantly advance safety and operational efficiency on construction sites.

By utilizing various communication channels, it will ensure timely responses to potential risks, thereby enabling proactive safety management and improving decision-making on-site.

## 3 DESIGN

## 3.1 Requirements for SSSS Components

According to Clause 27.29, here's a summary of the key requirements of SSSS Components:

- (1) Automation  and  Remote  Sensing:  The  Contractor  must  utilize  reliable  and  proven automation and remote sensing systems.
- (2) Accuracy in Alerts: Ensure SSSS components achieve at least 90% accuracy in detecting scenarios  that  trigger  alerts.  Live  trials  are  required  to  validate  this  accuracy,  with automated records of each warning signal transmitted to a control platform.
- (3) Video  Camera  Specifications:  Video  cameras  must  be  protected,  have  a  minimum resolution of 1080 pixels, at least 5X optical zoom, and transmit encrypted live-streaming video to the control platform.
- (4) Modification Procedures: Any interim modifications to SSSS components must minimize operational interruptions and require prior approval from the Project Manager.
- (5) Power Supply and Maintenance: Ensure adequate power supply for all components and perform necessary maintenance or repairs within 24 hours of reporting issues.
- (6) Data Management: All transmitted audio, video, and data must be digitized, time-stamped, available for API integration, and encrypted. The Project Manager should have access to this data upon request.
- (7) Data Security: Implement security measures to protect data integrity and confidentiality, regularly reviewing and upgrading security practices as needed.
- (8) Daily Checks: Assign qualified personnel to conduct daily checks on SSSS components to ensure proper functioning. Any irregularities must be recorded and corrected before work begins.
- (9) Action  Plan  for  Alerts:  Develop  an  action  plan  for  responding  to  alarms  or  unsafe conditions  generated  by  SSSS  components,  detailing  immediate  actions,  responsible personnel, and incident recording procedures.
- (10) Training  for  Personnel:  Provide  training  for  site  personnel  on  the  operation  of  SSSS components to ensure familiarity before work commencement.
- (11) Monthly  Reporting:  Submit  monthly  reports  on  SSSS  implementation  to  the  Project Manager before the Site Safety Management Committee meetings, including updates, incident summaries, and analyses of unsafe practices and alerts.

## 4 SITE TELECOMMUNICATION NETWORK AND INFRASTRUCTURE

All SSSS components including "Digitized tracking system for site plants, powered tools and ladders",  "Hazardous  Area  Access  Control  by  Electronic  Lock  and  Key  System",  "Smart Monitoring Devices for Workers and Frontline Site Personnel", and "Safety Monitoring System using  Artificial  Intelligence"  will  connect  to  internet  and  share  the  information  /  data  on Centralised Management Platform via 4G/5G network or Wi-Fi.

As the SSSS service provider, we will establish a site telecommunication network that can support mobile communication with adequate capabilities:

Data transmission speed : The network should have sufficiently high bandwidth and low latency to enable real-time transmission of data, including video/audio signals, from the automation/remote sensing devices to the target operators, receivers, monitors and data storage devices.

Data transmission capacity : The network should have enough capacity to handle the expected volume of data traffic from all the SSSS components without compromising performance.

Coverage :  The  network  coverage  should  be  comprehensive  across  the  entire  site  to  ensure seamless connectivity for all SSSS devices.

Connectivity :  The network should provide reliable and stable connections between the SSSS components and their control/monitoring systems.

Cybersecurity : Robust security measures should be implemented to protect the network and data transmissions from cyber threats and unauthorized access.

## 7 HAZARDOUS AREA ACCESS CONTROL BY ELECTRONIC LOCK AND KEY SYSTEM

Construction  sites  have  hazardous  areas  such  as  Electrical  switch  board  cabinets,  lift  shaft openings, and large floor openings. These areas will be secured using electronic equipment to prevent  unauthorized  access  and  to  stop  the  unlawful  opening  of  electrical  panels.  Only designated personnel, typically site supervisors, will hold authorized electronic keys with unique identities, allowing them to open the electronic locks.

The automated access control and warning system to prevent unauthorized opening of locked cover, doorway and barrier to hazardous areas within the Site which shall include but not limit to the following -

- (1) electrical distribution board cabinet;
- (2) floor opening equal to or larger than 500mm x 500mm entrance to confined space area; and
- (3) lift shaft opening.

The  access  control  system  will  include  an  electronic  lock  and  key  setup  designed  to  secure hazardous areas effectively. All electrical panels on site will be managed by the access control system  to  prevent  unauthorized  access.  These  electronic  locks  will  only  be  operable  with authorized electronic keys that have a key identity electronically embedded within them. Only designated personnel (i.e. authorized electricians and frontline supervisors) are allowed to hold the  specific  keys,  and  can  activate  and  unlock  the  electronic  locks  using  a  mobile  app  and biometric authentication. Designated site supervisors and authorized personnel (e.g. electricians) are issued unique electronic keys. The electronic keys are tied to the individual's identity and access  permissions.  The  keys  can  be  activated  and  deactivated  remotely  through  the  Central Management Platform (CMP).

The electronic lock system integrates with site sensors and the centralized safety platform. ## 16 ACTIONS / RESPONSES TO ALERTS FROM SSSS DEVICES AND COMMUNICATION

The  standardized  action  plan  comprises  5  parts  which  are  Immediate  Actions,  Response Procedures, Recommend Response Time, Responsible Personnel, and Incident Reporting. Immediate Actions:

- Upon receiving an alarm/alert from the SSSS components, the designated site safety personnel (e.g. Site Safety Officer) shall be notified immediately.
- The site safety personnel shall quickly assess the nature of the alert and determine if there is an immediate safety risk or hazardous condition that requires intervention.

## Response Procedures:

- If an unsafe act or hazardous condition is confirmed, the site safety personnel shall:
- (i) Initiate emergency response protocols as appropriate (e.g. evacuation, emergency equipment deployment)
- (ii) Dispatch  a  response  team  to  investigate  the  issue  and  implement  corrective measures
- (iii) Notify the site management team and project supervisors of the incident
- For non-critical alerts, the site safety personnel shall:
- (i) Dispatch a team to inspect the area and confirm the issue
- (ii) Determine appropriate corrective actions to address the identified safety concern
- (iii) Document the incident and remedial steps taken

## Recommend Response Time:

Within  5  minutes  of  alert  notification,  the  designated  site  safety  personnel  shall  initiate  any necessary emergency protocols, such as evacuations or emergency equipment deployment, if an immediate  safety  risk  or  hazardous  condition  is  confirmed. The  site  safety  personnel  shall dispatch a team to confirm the issue, and determine the appropriate corrective actions required to address the identified safety concern.

## Responsible Personnel:

Site Safety Officer - Primary responsible for coordinating response to SSSS alerts. Site Supervisors - Provide oversight and support for incident response and corrective actions.

Facilities  Maintenance  Team  -  Implement  technical  fixes  or  equipment  adjustments  as required.

## Incident Reporting:

All incidents triggered by the SSSS components shall be thoroughly documented, including:

- (a) Time and location of the incident

- (b) Description of the safety concern detected
- (c) Actions taken in response

The incident reports shall be submitted to the site management team for review.

This standardized action plan will ensure a rapid, coordinated, and well-documented response to SSSS-generated  alerts,  enabling  the  project  team  to  effectively  mitigate  safety  risks  and continually improve site safety practices.

## 为什么需要安装盲区行人智能产品

大型商用车辆（矿车、渣土车、叉车、货车、大巴等）视觉盲区范围大，在变道 和转弯过程中，碰到速度较快的车辆和突然出现的行人等情况，极易造成交通事 故的发生

## 行人&amp;车辆检测，智能驾驶辅助

通过摄像头捕捉盲区内画面，应用视觉测距计算出盲区的风险范围，并结合 人体检测识别等技术对获取图像进行处理分析，若在风险区域内检测到行人，则 向驾驶员发起警报。

能够实现智能，高精度的行人&amp;车辆检测，支持识别骑自行车行人、骑电动 车行人、徒步行人等各类行人和车辆，安全智能辅助司机驾驶，帮助避免大车、 工程车、叉车等各种车辆对行人的伤害。
